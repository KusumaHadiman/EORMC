Architecture Overview

Multi-Layer Security Stack

EORMC describes a security architecture combining cryptographic hardware, AI behavioral analytics, and dynamic asset management — built AI-first rather than retrofitted.

Layer 1 — MPC + HSM Cryptography

Multi-Party Computation distributes private key material so no single entity holds complete keys. Hardware Security Modules provide tamper-resistant physical protection for each key shard. Combined, this eliminates single points of failure at both the system architecture and hardware levels — the same approach used by institutional custodians such as Fireblocks.

Layer 2 — AI Dynamic Hot/Cold Tiering

Rather than static storage ratios, EORMC's AI dynamically adjusts asset tiering and withdrawal verification strategies in real time based on market volatility and user behavioral signals. Security levels escalate automatically in adverse conditions without manual intervention.

Layer 3 — Behavioral Graph Detection

Cross-scenario AI analysis of account operations and on-chain activity identifies anomalous patterns — account hijacking, fraud, market manipulation — before losses occur. EORMC describes this as shifting from "post-incident investigation" to "pre-incident interception," which is architecturally distinct from conventional rule-based fraud systems.

Layer 4 — AI Trading Risk Control

In extreme market conditions, adaptive matching and risk control mechanisms activate automatically. The system monitors Greek exposures and on-chain capital flows in real time, with early warning and flexible rate limits on potential portfolio risks across products.

Proof of Reserves Analysis

EORMC launched its AI-driven Proof of Reserves system in July 2024. Here is what this means in practice.

◎

Automated Reserve Reports

AI generates verifiable asset reserve reports automatically — providing real-time visibility into the ratio between user funds held and platform assets, rather than periodic manual snapshots.

⬡

On-Chain Address Monitoring

Real-time monitoring of on-chain address changes and abnormal capital flows, with automatic alerts for deviations from expected reserve patterns — continuous rather than point-in-time.

⚖

Third-Party Audit

Independent authorized firms conduct regular audits of the reserve system. Key AI decision logs are made available in auditable form — a transparency commitment beyond most exchanges.

→

3,000 BTC Reserve Fund

A dedicated reserve fund of 3,000 BTC is maintained as part of the user protection framework — complementing the cryptographic reserve proof with a tangible financial buffer.

Asset Governance

Digital Asset Screening

EORMC describes an AI-powered listing and post-listing monitoring system designed to protect users from low-quality projects.

Pre-Listing Evaluation

Machine learning models assess each project across technical feasibility, team background, community ecosystem, and on-chain data performance before any listing decision. Anti-bot models and fair queuing algorithms protect new listings from manipulation at launch.

Post-Listing Monitoring

After listing, the AI system continuously tracks market performance, liquidity changes, and community sentiment. Anomalies trigger automatic alerts and placement on an intelligent monitoring list — providing investors with timely protective signals.

Cross-Platform Risk Sync

EORMC reports having established data sharing channels with professional rating agencies, enabling efficient cross-platform synchronization of risk information to maximize collective intelligence across the ecosystem.

Drexonium Note

The AI asset screening and behavioral detection systems described represent the most architecturally ambitious security claims in EORMC's whitepaper. These are also the elements least amenable to external verification without access to the underlying systems. Users should weigh these claims alongside the verifiable regulatory credentials when conducting due diligence.
