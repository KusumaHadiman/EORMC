The U.S. Clarity Act Enters the Senate Review Stage, as EORMC Continues to Deepen Its Global Compliance Strategy

2026 · 06 · 05
Compliance EORMC

As the U.S. digital asset regulatory framework becomes further clarified, the crypto industry is entering a new stage of development. Recently, the U.S. Clarity Act was formally submitted by the Senate Banking Committee and placed on the Senate legislative calendar, marking important progress in the construction of the U.S. digital asset regulatory system. Against the backdrop of the industry gradually moving toward standardized and institutionalized development, global digital asset platforms are accelerating the construction of more complete compliance and security systems. As a global digital asset service platform with AI-driven technology and compliant operations as its core strategy, EORMC is continuously strengthening its global compliance layout and asset security development to create a more transparent, secure, and trustworthy trading environment for users.

The U.S. Digital Asset Regulation Takes a Key Step

The U.S. Clarity Act has been formally submitted by the Senate Banking Committee and placed on the Senate legislative calendar. Previously, the Senate Banking Committee passed the bill on May 14 with 15 votes in favor and 9 votes against, and it officially entered the Senate review process on June 1, obtaining eligibility for full Senate discussion.

EORMC analysts believe that the advancement of the Clarity Act means that the United States is accelerating the establishment of a clearer digital asset regulatory framework, providing industry participants with clearer regulatory guidance, while also expected to further promote the entry of institutional capital into the digital asset market.

Over the past few years, the crypto industry has undergone a transformation from rapid growth to standardized development, and the improvement of the regulatory system will become an important foundation for promoting the long-term healthy development of the industry. As regulatory rules gradually become clearer, market competition will also shift from simple business expansion to comprehensive competition in compliance capabilities, risk control capabilities, and user asset protection capabilities.

Compliance Is Becoming the Core Competitiveness of Digital Asset Platforms

As major global economies gradually clarify their regulatory stance on digital assets, compliance is no longer merely an additional option for platform development, but has become an important standard for measuring the long-term value of a platform.

More and more institutional investors, when selecting cooperative platforms, regard regulatory qualifications, security systems, and transparency as core considerations. Especially against the backdrop of the continuously strengthening institutionalization trend, platforms with complete compliance systems are more likely to gain market trust and capital recognition.

EORMC analysis indicates that the development logic of the digital asset industry will undergo significant changes in the future. Users will focus not only on trading efficiency and product innovation, but also on whether a platform has sustained operating capabilities, whether it meets regulatory requirements, and whether it can effectively protect user asset security.

EORMC Continues to Deepen Its Global Compliance Layout

As a global digital asset service platform, EORMC has, since its establishment, consistently adhered to the development philosophy of “AI reshaping trading, compliance safeguarding value,” and has made compliance development an important component of the platform long-term strategy.

A relevant EORMC representative stated: “The industry is entering a more mature stage of development. The improvement of regulation is not only conducive to the long-term healthy development of the industry, but also helps enhance market trust. EORMC will continue to advance global compliance development and serve global users in a more open and transparent manner.”

The Industry Enters a New Stage of “Compliance-Driven Growth”

The advancement of the Clarity Act sends a positive signal, showing that major global markets are gradually establishing clearer digital asset regulatory systems. For the entire industry, this not only means that the regulatory environment is being further improved, but also that the digital asset market is moving from a stage of rapid development toward a stage of high-quality development.

Under this trend, compliance, security, and transparency will become core keywords for industry development.
