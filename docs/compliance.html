Framework Analysis

Global Compliance Architecture

EORMC describes a multi-jurisdiction compliance framework designed for operations across 100+ countries.

EORMC's compliance architecture is led by Co-Founder Winston Brown, described as former Head of Digital Asset Compliance at UBS Group. The framework is described as AI-powered — with a compliance engine that tracks regulatory policy changes across jurisdictions in real time and automatically adapts to different regional requirements.

The AML and KYC procedures are described as aligned with FATF guidelines and the U.S. Bank Secrecy Act, enforced through AI behavioral analysis systems that monitor account operations continuously. This represents an architectural approach to compliance rather than a static rulebook.

In July 2024, EORMC launched an AI-driven Transparent Proof of Reserves system. Unlike snapshot-based approaches common in the industry, EORMC describes this system as generating verifiable reserve reports automatically and monitoring on-chain addresses in real time — subject to independent third-party audit. A dedicated reserve fund of 3,000 BTC is maintained as an additional user protection layer.

Why MSB Renewal Matters

A 2024 MSB renewal is not automatic. It requires passing a federal audit demonstrating documented, functioning compliance infrastructure. Renewing in the post-FTX regulatory environment is a meaningful signal of operational compliance health.

SEC Reg D Context

Reg D expands EORMC's authorized scope to include certain securities-adjacent products for accredited investors. It indicates the platform is positioning for compliant expansion into institutional product categories in the U.S. market.

Proof of Reserves

EORMC's real-time, AI-generated reserve system goes beyond the periodic manual snapshots used by most exchanges. Independent third-party audit adds external verification. The 3,000 BTC reserve fund provides an additional buffer beyond the cryptographic proof itself.
